  1. The Kline family descends from Germany, around the Saarland, Wurttemberg, and Prussia. They immigrated to the colony of Pennsylvania in the 18th century.

  2. The Farner family descends from the canton of Zurich in Switzerland. They immigrated to the colony of Pennsylvania in the 18th century.

  3. The Ammerman family descends from the province of Holland in the Netherlands. They immigrated to New Amsterdam (modern-day New York City) in about 1650. The immigrant, Dirck Jansen Ammerman, had a prominent role here. For example, he commanded a foot company in the Canada expedition of 1691.

  4. The Yingst family descends from Germany, around Hesse-Nassau and Prussia. They immigrated to the colony of Pennsylvania in the 18th century.

  5. The Price family descends from Wales and the Isle of Wight, immigrating to the colony of Maryland (around Anne Arundel) in the 17th century. They moved to Pennsylvania in the late 18th century. They were mainly farmers, and owned a farm on the Clay Township-Cromwell Township border, where a family cemetery can be found.

  6. The Simondale family descends from Italy, around Rome. They immigrated to Pennsylvania in the early 20th century.

  7. The Moleski family descends from Poland. They immigrated to Pennsylvania in the late 19th century, probably hired by a coal company.

  8. The Carpenter family descends from the United Kingdom (specifically England), and immigrated to the colony of new York in the 17th century. The family from which I am descended is the Rehoboth Branch. This family is particularly notable because they were very influential in the printing business and in local politics, gaining a lot of wealth.

  9. It is important to note that I, the author of this webpage, also have Irish heritage through the Mullarkey family.
